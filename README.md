## Features That Actually Matter

**Paper Trading** — You can run the bot with fake money against real market data. This is genuinely useful before you risk actual capital on a configuration you're not sure about.

**Backtesting** — Test how your strategy would have performed using historical data. It's not a guarantee of future results, but it's a much better starting point than guessing.

**Strategy Marketplace** — Strategies, templates, and signal subscriptions from other traders. Some are free, many are paid. The quality varies, so paper-trading a purchased strategy before going live is the right move.

**Copy Bot** — Available even on the free Pioneer plan. You pick a trader, connect your exchange, and the bot mirrors their activity. Good starting point if you're new and don't want to build strategies from scratch yet.

**Trailing Orders** — Instead of setting a fixed sell point, the trailing stop moves with the price and sells when it reverses. Useful in momentum markets where flat stop-losses get you out too early.

**DCA (Dollar Cost Averaging)** — Automatically buys more of a position as the price drops, lowering your average cost. A popular risk management tool.

**Exchange Support** — Binance, Coinbase, Kraken, KuCoin, Bybit, OKX, Bitfinex, Bitstamp, Gemini, and others. One dashboard, multiple exchanges.

## What Real Users Say

The honest picture from reviews across Trustpilot, G2, and community forums is a bit of a mixed bag — which is about right for any platform of this complexity.

On the positive side, users frequently mention the **depth of features**, the **marketplace as a useful shortcut**, and the fact that the bot runs without interruption. One recurring comment is that people who put in the time to learn the platform see results; those who expect a plug-and-play profit machine get frustrated.

The common criticisms: the onboarding has a learning curve, the UI isn't perfectly intuitive (Cryptohopper themselves have acknowledged this and are working on redesigns), and some users have reported configuration issues where the bot didn't behave as expected — usually traceable to settings problems rather than bugs.

Support response times get mixed reviews too — some find it quick and helpful, others report delays. For complicated cases, that 24-hour response target they aim for can stretch longer on weekends.

Net impression: it's a legitimate, established platform that's been running since 2017 with a large user base. It rewards people who approach it as a tool to learn, not a magic box.
